Kamal Acharya (कमल आचार्य; born 27 August 1998) is a researcher and author from Nepal who works in the field of computer science and information technology (CSIT)[1][2]. He is interested in many areas including project management, science and technology, computer engineering, writing research articles, and using tools like Google Analytics to understand data[3]. Kamal studies how technology can be used to solve real-life problems and develops projects and software systems for schools, businesses, and other organizations. He has published several academic papers and project reports that show his skills in applying knowledge to practical work. He is active on platforms like SSRN, ResearchGate, ORCID, and Google Scholar, where he shares his research and connects with other researchers. His work helps improve understanding of technology and inspires students and professionals in Nepal and beyond. [4]

Early life and education

‎Kamal Acharya was born on August 27, 1998, in the Kailali district of Nepal, where he was raised in a supportive family by his parents, Dilli Raj Acharya and Sharada Devi Acharya,[5] who encouraged his academic growth from an early age; he completed his early schooling at Janapriya Secondary School in Kailali, earning his School Leaving Certificate (SLC) in 2014 with a strong focus on science subjects, after which he pursued his Intermediate of Science (I.Sc.) at Radiant Secondary School in Mahendranagar from 2014 to 2016, further strengthening his foundation in scientific and technical studies, and later continued his higher education by enrolling in a Bachelor’s program in Computer Science and Information Technology (CSIT) under Farwestern University and Master's program in Computer Science and Information Technology (CSIT) under Tribhuvan University, where he developed deeper knowledge in computing, programming, and information systems, shaping his academic and professional direction in the field of technology. [6]

Kamal Acharya married Babita Baduwal (now Babita Acharya) on July 6, 2020, in a relatively private and low-key ceremony. The wedding occurred during the early months of the COVID-19 pandemic in Nepal, which likely limited gatherings, guests and traditional grand celebrations.Babita Baduwal (now Babita Acharya) was born on December 20, 1998, in Kanchanpur district, Nepal. She completed her Intermediate in Management from Sunrise Public School and earned a Bachelor of Management degree from Tribhuvan University. She is fluent in English and Nepali. Professionally, Babita works as a digital content creator, producer, and social worker. She has produced short lifestyle vlogs and videos, including “My First Vlog | Together Vlog” and content on the lifestyle of hilly region people (released in 2022). She maintains a low-profile presence on platforms like Facebook as a digital creator[7].

Teaching Experience

Kamal Acharya has gained valuable teaching experience in the field of Computer Science and Engineering at both lower and higher secondary levels. During his time at Japanpriya Secondary School, Likma, Kailali (2022–2023), he focused on teaching in a clear and simple way so students could easily understand computer concepts. He taught subjects like basic programming, computer hardware, and software applications by using examples, demonstrations, and small classroom projects. He encouraged students to practice regularly and ask questions, which helped them become more confident in using computers. His teaching style was friendly and supportive, making the classroom environment comfortable for learning.

Earlier, at Birendra Secondary School, Ghangal, Doti (2019–2022), Kamal Acharya worked with students from different backgrounds and learning abilities. He used simple explanations and step-by-step methods to teach difficult topics, ensuring that all students could follow along. He connected computer education to real-life situations so students could understand how technology is useful in daily life. Along with teaching, he also helped improve basic computer skills among students and supported other teachers in using digital tools. His dedication and practical teaching approach helped create interest in computer education and improved students’ overall learning experience.
